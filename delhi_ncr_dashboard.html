Methodology & Feature Definitions

Supply Stock

Count of distinct property_ids with event_type ≠ 'deleted' at a given scrape date. Captures the full visible inventory on the platform at that moment.

  • New supply velocity — listings where event_type = 'new'
  • Stale inventory — listings unchanged across ≥2 consecutive runs
  • Developer supply — listings with a non-null developer_name

Absorption (Demand Proxy)

Property IDs present in run N but absent in run N+1 are treated as absorbed (likely sold or de-listed). Only same-source consecutive runs within 21 days are compared.

absorbed ≈ max(0, prev_seen + curr_new − curr_seen)
absorption_rate = absorbed_weekly / active_supply × 100
MOI = active_supply / (absorbed_weekly × 4.33)

Market Tightness Score

Composite indicator for pricing direction. Values above 1 signal upward price pressure; below 0.5 indicate oversupply.

Tightness = (Absorption Rate / New Supply Velocity) × (1 / MOI)

> 1.0 → Seller's market (demand > supply)
0.5–1.0 → Balanced
< 0.5 → Buyer's market

Price Momentum (DB mode)

Detected when event_type = 'updated' and price_numeric changed between runs. Requires PostgreSQL access.

price_momentum = [(hikes − cuts) / total_updated]
                  × avg_sqft_price_Δ

Positive → net hikes (demand confidence)
Negative → net cuts (supply pressure)
